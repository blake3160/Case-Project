About RPGs

A role playing game can take on many forms, but the general idea is the same: a game in which one plays the role of a character other than themself, usually in a fantasy setting. Following the breakout success of J.R.R. Tolkien's popular works of fiction, the fantasy genre was formed. It comes as no surprise that when video games became a pastime, fantasy soon became involved.

Early role playing games, or RPGs, started simple as most genres are known to have done. Adventure for the Atari 2600 had a narrative in its manual, but only demonstrated in game a little square gliding through mazes and avoiding strange creatures so as to acquire a chalice.

Later on, as video games developed better hardware, RPGs became more complex. Chrono Trigger for the Super Nintendo had a wide array of sound effects and colors, an in game narrative, characters with written dialogue and systems for engaging with friendly non-player characters or unfriendly monsters.

Contemporary RPGs now feature much more than those of the past, including voiced characters, explorable territory which can span hundreds of kilometers and fully-animated, three-dimensional models which interact with their worlds.
